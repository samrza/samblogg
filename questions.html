Questions

Deep questions I've wrestled with and my current thoughts.

What does it mean to live a good life?

This question has been on my mind a lot lately. I used to think it was about achieving certain milestones - career success, financial stability, relationships. But I'm starting to believe it's more about the quality of your daily experience and the impact you have on others.

Current thoughts: A good life might be one where you're constantly growing, helping others, and finding meaning in your work. It's not about the destination but the journey and how you show up each day.

How do we balance technology and human connection?

As someone who builds technology products, I often wonder about the impact of our digital tools on human relationships. Are we creating more connection or just the illusion of it?

Current thoughts: Technology should enhance, not replace, human connection. It's about using tools mindfully and being present in our interactions, whether digital or in-person.

What's the role of discomfort in growth?

I've noticed that my biggest periods of growth have come from challenging situations. But how do we know when to push through discomfort and when to step back?

Current thoughts: Discomfort is a signal, not a stop sign. It's about learning to distinguish between the discomfort of growth and the discomfort of something that's truly wrong for you.

How do we maintain authenticity in a world of social media?

In an age where we can curate our online presence, how do we stay true to ourselves while still being mindful of our digital footprint?

Current thoughts: Authenticity isn't about sharing everything, but about being honest in what we do share. It's about aligning our online presence with our true values and beliefs.
